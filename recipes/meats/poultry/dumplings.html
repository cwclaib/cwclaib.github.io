Dumplings - Poultry

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups sifted flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt (unless broth is salty)
  • 1 well beaten egg
  • 1 cup boiling broth
  • stewed chicken or other meat

Instructions:

  1. Sift the flour, baking powder, and salt together in bowl.
  2. Pour the boiling broth over flour mixture, let stand until cold, and add the beaten egg
    1. If the dough is too soft to roll, add more flour; if it is too stiff, add some sweet milk
  3. Roll and cut in your desired shape and thickness. Drop into boiling brother. Cover and cook 12-15 minutes.

My grandmother, Mrs. Mollie Henley Jonas, who was born March 17, 1851 in Wythe County, Virginia, used the following recipe. My mother, Bessie Jonas Johnson, has used this recipe for 48 years and I have used it for 7 years. We use it mainly for dumplings with stewed chicken but can be used with any meat.

Source: Mrs. Meredith L. Richardson, Alleghany County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
