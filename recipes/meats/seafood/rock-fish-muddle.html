Rock Fish Muddle - Seafood

Ingredients:

  • 30 lbs fish
  • 3-½ lbs bacon fat, cubed
  • 3-½ lbs onions

Instructions:

  1. Fry bacon brown and take bacon and grease out of pot
  2. Put in fish heads with insides to pot; add in brown bacon and grease as you put in fish
  3. Lay flesh side of fish in pot -- do not salt until cooking has started
  4. Add water to about cover fish, and add more if needed while cooking
  5. Add salt and pepper to taste at any time
  6. One hour before dish is finished, add onions -- never stir until ready to serve
  7. Cook for 2-½ to 4 hours according to size of muddle
  8. Cook slow for first half hour, then hard for next two hours, then slow.

Handed down by Mr. Harvey Lassiter of Lasker, North Carolina, in 1944. Mr. Lassiter is now deceased. Perhaps this recipe has been used in the town of Lasker for many years since this little town is noted for getting together for feeds.

Note: Northampton County is famous for Rock Muddle since the Roanoke River is so near. This is definitely a Northampton dish. In early spring everyone turns out for county and community "Rock Muddles". It is different from and better than stewed fish.

Source: Mrs. T. T. Stephenson, Northampton County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
