Tidewater Virginia Pickled Oysters - Seafood

Ingredients:

  • Oysters **
  • vinegar (1 qt per pint of oyster liquor)
  • whole cloves (1 tbsp per pint etc)
  • black pepper (1 tbsp per pint etc)
  • mace (1 tsp per pint etc)
  • butter

Instructions:

  1. Strain liquor from oysters and to every pint add 1 quart of vinegar, 1 tbsp whole cloves, 1 tbsp black pepper, and 1 tsp mace.
  2. Heat liquor and vinegar, then add spices and bring to boiling
  3. Add oysters and allow to come to boiling point. Once cooked, add a lump of butter the size of an egg
  4. Serve ice cold with a turkey dinner.

This recipe was handed to me by my grandmother, Mrs. Leonard Hyslop, who was Miss Georgeanna Pullen before marriage. It was handed down to Grandmother Hyslop by her grandmother (my great, great grandmother) who reared her. Her own mother having died during the yellow fever epidemic that struck Portsmouth, Virginia, before the Civil War.

** From Pickled Oysters, p. 58, 1 gallon oysters requires 1.5 pints vinegar, so assuming the ratio is similar, you would need 5.25 pints or 21/32 gallons of oysters per pint of vinegar

Source: Mrs. W. R. Grant, Montgomery County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
