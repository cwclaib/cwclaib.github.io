Sea Turtle Hash - Seafood

NOTE: it is currently illegal to hunt or eat sea turtles in the United States

Ingredients:

  • 1 small(er) sea turtle
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 4 medium onions
  • Salt pork
  • 1 cup + 1 quart water
  • 4 lbs diced white potatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Chop off the turtle’s head. Cut the complete legs away from the shell and entrails.
  2. Scald legs with boiling water; remove outside layer of skin and nails.
  3. Rinse legs in cold water, then place whole legs in pressure cooker with 1 quart of water and a teaspoon of soda.
  4. Process at 15 lbs pressure for 30 minutes, then remove turtle legs from water and cool.
  5. Dice 1/4 lb salt pork into small pieces and fry in a heavy cook pot.
  6. Remove salt pork, slightly saute 4 medium onions in salt pork fat.
  7. Add to pressure cooker salt pork, 1 cup of water, 4 lbs diced white potatoes, boneless turtle legs, diced (including fatback and gristle), and salt and pepper. Cover and cook until potatoes are tender and gravy is down to medium

Sea turtle hash is definitely a “he man’s” dish from the briny deep; and this recipe has been handed down in my family of Cape Hatteras fisherman for several generations from the sailing vessel era when men were men, but may be enjoyed without prejudice, by the entire family. My mother prepared it in an old-fashioned, fireplace style, iron pot over a wood fire; and I don’t claim to compete with her for flavor and aroma but my modern pressure cooker certainly saves time.

Take any sea turtle you are lucky enough to get but the following ingredients are for a smaller size sea turtle. If your turtle is too large for one meal, by all means can or freeze it for a future feast.

Source: Mrs. E. N. Elliott, Chowan County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
