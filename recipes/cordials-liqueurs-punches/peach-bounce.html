Peach/Cherry Bounce - Cordials, Liqueurs, & Punches


(The recipe is formatted for cherry bounce, since that is the way I first came across it, however peach bounce is my preference)

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound cherries, stemmed
    • Bing cherries will be sweet, others tart
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 4 cups bourbon (peach) or vodka (cherry) (or rum, cognac, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Pierce each cherry with the tip of a knife in 1 or 2 places.
  2. Combine the sugar and 1 cup of vodka in a 1/2 gal. jar and shake to dissolve sugar.
  3. Add fruit, then shake again, then top with vodka.
  4. Shake gently to distribute the fruit.
  5. Place the jar in a sunny indoor spot; let it sit for 1 week, then transfer it to a dark spot and let it sit for 40 days. The color of the bounce will darken/intensify.
  6. Seat a strainer over a pitcher or container with a pour spout. Strain the cherry mixture; reserve or discard the fruit.
  7. Cover the strained liquor and let it settle for a few hours, then pour the bounce into a clean 1-quart jar
  8. NOTE: peach bounce: choose bourbon. Add a dozen coin-size slices of fresh ginger root. Peel and chop the peaches before infusing.

Serves 16

I've made both the cherry and peach version of this bounce recipe; the peach is fair and away my favorite. The ginger works very well with the peach and bourbon flavors. The cherry one is good but can taste a bit medicinal. I used about half bing cherries and half was Ranier cherries for the cherry bounce, and used all yellow peaches for the peach bounce.
