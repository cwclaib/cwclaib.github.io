Old-Fashioned Light Bread - Baking

Ingredients:

  • 3 coffee cups (24 oz) fresh, sweet milk
  • 3 coffee cups (24 oz) boiling water
  • One large spoon (1-1/2 tbsp) yellow sugar or good molasses
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsp cornmeal
  • Enough flour to make a thin batter
  • 1 piece of lard

Instructions:

  1. Pour into a tin bucket 3 coffee cups of fresh milk and 3 coffee cups of boiling water
  2. Separately, add 1 large spoon yellow sugar or good molasses, one tablespoon salt, two tablespoons cornmeal, and enough flour to make a thin batter.
    1. Be sure the milk and water have cooled to lukewarm before adding flour or it will lump.
  3. Pour out one half the milk and water, make a thick batter, add other half of milk and water. Mixed this way you have a smooth thin batter.
  4. Place in pail of warm water, stirring several times before it starts to rise, must be kept a little more than milk warm until it starts to rise one half its original height.
  5. Add a little piece of lard and make into a dough about like biscuit dough.
  6. Put in pans in 3 loaves. The heat should be just warm enough to bear the hand on. Keep this heat till it has risen as high as you want it. Bake.

Instead of a tin and yellow sugar, a mixing bowl and white sugar may be substituted
