Carter's Cornbread - Baking

Ingredients:

  • Dry ingredients:
    • 1 C corn flour (not wheat flour)
    • 1 C corn meal
    • 1/2 tbsp salt
    • 1 and a 1/4 tbsp baking powder
    • 1/2 tbsp baking soda
  • Wet ingredients
    • 1 C corn
    • 1 C buttermilk (at room temperature)
    • 2 eggs at room temperature
    • 1/4 C unsalted butter (If you use salted butter, cut the dry ingredient salt to 1/4 from 1/2 tbsp)
    • 1/4 C white sugar
    • 1/4 C brown sugar

Instructions:

  1. Mix the dry ingredients, then the wet ingredients.
  2. Then pour the dry into the wet and mix.
  3. Use a 9" x 9" baking pan and use butter to lube the pan. Note that the pan needs to have sides that are as vertical as possible, not slanted. (It makes a big difference in the final thickness of the ingredients)
  4. Put pan into a preheated 400 F oven. Use the middle rack and bake for about 23 minutes--keep an eye on it starting at 20 minutes.

Primarily based on the recipe from Foods That Rate at NC State (link), this variation was written in collaboration with a friend, B.M., who would prefer to remain anonymous. The excellent commentary and notes are his doing, as is the name. He also suggested using a ratio of baking powder to soda that was closer to 1:1.
