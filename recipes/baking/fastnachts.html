Fastnachts (Fosnoxs) - Baking

Ingredients:

  • 1 package yeast
  • 1/2 cup shortening
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1-1/2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • enough flour to make soft dough

Instructions:

  1. Scald milk; when cool, add yeast, shortening, sugar, salt, egg, and flour. Mix well until light and smooth.
  2. Put in warm place and let rise until it doubles in bulk.
  3. Roll out into six inch circles as for pastry shell. Slash with knife, into 3/4” strips to within 3/4” of circles edge.
  4. Pick up first strip and loop second through first -- continue across circle.
  5. Let rise again on clean cloth until double in size.
  6. Fry in deep fat until golden brown. Drain on paper.

This recipe dates back four generations to Civil War days. It was always made and served on Shrove Tuesday in our family. Tradition was that this was mandatory if one wished to have success growing cabbage that year. Also some must be fed the hens to assure heavy laying that year. At our hours the hens was fed only in token portions.

Fastnachts are delicious served hot with fruit preserve and coffee or with a salad course.

[[This meal is traditionally Pennsylvania Dutch]]
