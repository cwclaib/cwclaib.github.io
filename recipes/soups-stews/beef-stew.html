Beef Stew - Soups and Stews

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 1 pound beef stewing meat (or beef shank), trimmed and cut into inch cubes
  • 5 teaspoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 tbsp herbs de Provence
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/2 tbsp. Worcestershire sauce
  • 3-1/2 cups beef broth, homemade or low-sodium canned
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and chopped
  • 5 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1/4-inch rounds
  • 2 large baking potatoes, peeled and cut into 3/4-inch cubes
  • 2 teaspoons salt

Instructions:

  1. Prep
    1. Combine the flour and pepper in a bowl/paper bag, add the beef and toss to coat well
    2. Heat 3 teaspoons of the oil in a large pot. Add the beef a few pieces at a time; do not overcrowd.
    3. Cook, turning the pieces until beef is browned on all sides, about 5 minutes per batch; add more oil as needed between batches.
  2. Sauce
    1. Remove the beef from the pot and add the vinegar, wine, herbs de provence, tomato paste, and Worcestershire Sauce. Cook over medium-high heat, scraping the pan with a wooden spoon to loosen any browned bits.
    2. Add the beef, beef broth and bay leaves. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a slow simmer.
  3. Simmer
    1. Cover and cook, skimming broth from time to time, until the beef is tender, about 1-1/2 hours.
    2. Add the onions and carrots and simmer, covered, for 10 minutes.
    3. Add the potatoes and simmer until vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes more.
    4. Add broth or water if the stew is dry. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Ladle among 4 bowls and serve.

Source: The New York Times - Old-Fashioned Beef Stew
