Old-Fashioned Potato Salad - Sides and Sauces

Ingredients:

  • 8 medium-to-large cured Irish potatoes
  • 1 scant tsp prepared mustard
  • 3+ tbsp vinegar
  • 1 medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste (it takes a good deal)

Instructions:

  1. Peel and boil potatoes in salt water until tender
  2. Mash drained potatoes while hot.
  3. Chop one of the hard-boiled eggs, then add it, mustard, vinegar, onion, salt, and pepper to potatoes. Beat well with fork. Taste for additional seasonings
  4. Put into serving dish and garnish top with other egg, either sliced or chopped fine.

This recipe has been handed down by word of mouth. My mother, Mrs. Neill McKimmon, Flora McNeill McEachern, who lives in Red Springs, North Carolina, often uses it, and my family likes it too. It was passed to her by her mother, Mrs. John H. McEachern, who was Miss Mary Ann McNeill before marriage. This lady, my grandmother, was grown and had already finished at old Floral College when the Civil War began.

It is nice made the day before needed, put into baking dish, and re-heated for serving

Source: Mrs. David H. Harris, Montgomery County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
