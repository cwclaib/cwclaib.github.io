Boston Baked Beans - Sides and Sauces

Ingredients:

  • two generous cups dry beans
  • 6” x 8” square of salt pork
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup molasses
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 tsp dry mustard

Instructions:

  1. Soak beans overnight and put on to boil early in the morning. When the skins curl off they have boiled long enough.
  2. Put salt pork in bottom of bean pot or iron kettle with a tight fitting lid and place on top of it the remaining ingredients except the beans.
  3. Put beans on top of the above mixture, and enough hot water to cover only.
  4. Cook in oven slowly from 9:30 [AM] until supper time.

Mrs. Lassie Kelly, operator of Kelly’s Tea Room in Franklin and sister of the well known North Carolina worker of adult education, Miss Elizabeth Kelly, is known for her good food to the many tourists who pass through North Carolina. One of the several dishes is Boston Baked Beans. The Kelly’s love for reading explains how the recipe came about. Miss Kelly found it in the book, “We Took to the Woods” by Louise Dickinson Rich. She tried the recipe and liked it so much that she has used it for a number of years.

Source: Mrs. Alice Ray, Macon County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
