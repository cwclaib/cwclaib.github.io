Apple Pan Dowdy - Desserts

(See also: Bunglet and Pandowdy)

Ingredients:

  • 3 apples, sliced
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 cup cake flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup melted butter or margarine
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1/2 tsp grated lemon rind
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions:

  1. Grease a round or square cake pan, about 8 or 9 inches in diameter.
  2. Wash apples, pare, cut in quarters, core, and slice. There should be 2 cups sliced apples when finished.
  3. Place apples in a baking dish, spreading them evenly over the surface and putting them in neatly. Sprinkle with brown sugar.
  4. Measure melted butter into mixing bowl. Stir in sugar gradually, add egg and beat it into ingredients vigorously.
  5. Have flour sifted, measured, and sifted with baking powder and salt. Add it alternately to first mixture, with milk, stirring to mix well after each addition.
  6. When last has been added, add lemon rind and juice, stirring it in.
  7. Turn over apples and brown sugar.
  8. Bake in a moderate oven of 350 degrees F. until when the cake is tested with a cake tester or toothpick it comes out perfectly clean.
  9. Remove from oven and remove at once from pan to serving platter.
  10. Serve warm with plain or whipped cream or Sunshine Sauce or Lemon Sauce (no recipe given).

Serves 5 or 6.

Source: Wartime Cooking and Canning Book (c. 1943)
