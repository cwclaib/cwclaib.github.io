Julia Ross' Moravian Sugar Cake - Desserts

Ingredients:

  • Cake
    • 1 or 2 packages of dry yeast (see alternate method below)
    • 1/2 tsp granulated sugar
    • 1 cup warm (105-110 deg) water
    • 3 cups unbleached all purpose flour, leveled
    • 2 tbsp dry skim milk powder
    • 3 or 6 tbsp dehydrated potatoes (3 for finely ground, 6 flakes)
    • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs, room temp
    • 1/2 cup butter or margarine, softened
  • Frosting
    • 1 tbsp light cream or milk
    • ⅔ cup brown sugar, packed
    • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 cup butter/margarine, melted and cooled

Instructions:

  1. Sprinkle years and 1/2 tsp sugar onto water in large mixing bowl.
  2. When dissolved and beginning to show signs of life, add all other ingredients except 1 cup of flour
  3. Beat on medium speed of a mixer for 5 minutes
  4. With a spoon add the other cup of flour to make a soft dough. Cover and place in a warm spot to rise until doubled in bulk (about 1-1/2 hours)
  5. Beat down and let rise again if you have time (about 45 minutes)
  6. Now turn dough into greased baking pan 17” x 11” x 1” and use hands to spread evenly over entire surface. Cover and let rise until light (1 hour)
  7. When dough is light, brush with cream.
  8. Combine the sugar and cinnamon and sprinkle evenly over entire surface of the dough.
  9. With fingertips make shallow indentations in dough 1” apart
  10. Sprinkle butter over all. Bake for 15 minutes in a 375 degree oven.

Makes 20 generous servings

(Alternate method for overnight rising)

Late in the day before you need sugar cake the following day, make dough as above, except use 2 pkgs. yeast. After rising in the bowl once (about 1 hour), stir down, cover with foil and place in refrigerator. A half hour later, punch down because it will have risen again before the dough became sufficiently cool to retard yeast growth. Refrigerate overnight. Next morning, place in pan and let rise until light (about 1-1/2 hours)
