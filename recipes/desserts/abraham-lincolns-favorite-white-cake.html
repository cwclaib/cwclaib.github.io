Abraham Lincoln's Favorite White Cake - Desserts

Ingredients:

  • 1 cupfull of butter
  • 2 cupfulls of sugar
  • 1 cupfull of milk
  • 3 cupfulls of flour
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • whites of 6 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla (or other flavoring, as preferred)
  • 1 cupfull of chopped blanched almonds
  • boiled icing
  • 1/2 cupfull candied pineapples and cherries, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cream butter well, add sugar and cream again
  2. Sift flour and baking powder together, add to butter and sugar alternately with milk
  3. Then stir in chopped nut meats and beat well
  4. Finally fold in the stiffly beaten whites of 6 eggs, add flavoring
  5. Pour into a well-greased, paper-lined pan and bake 1 hour in a moderate oven (350)
  6. Ice with boiled icing to which you have added half a cupful of candied pineapples and cherries cut in very small pieces

Long ago a French caterer in Lexington, Kentucky made a wonderful white cake in honor of his countryman, Lafayette, who was to pay a visit to the city. The cake was beautifully decorated with flags made of colored sugar and with marvelous icing, but the cake itself contained only the whites of eggs, and when cut was snow-white. Thereupon every famous cake baker in the Blue-Grass region immediately began making white cakes; and the recipe for the most famous of all was originated in the household of the ancestors of Mary Todd, who many years afterward made the cake for Abraham Lincoln. Later when she became his wife, he declared this white cake was the best in Kentucky. Here is Mary Todd's recipe with modern baking powder included.

Source: Margaret Renigar, Yadkin County - From North Carolina Kitchens: Favorite Recipes Old and New (1957)
