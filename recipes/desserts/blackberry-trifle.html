Blackberry Trifle - Desserts

Ingredients:

  • 1 qt blackberries
  • 3 eggs, seperated
  • 4 heaping tbsp sugar + 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1 heaping tbsp cornstarch
  • Small piece of butter to grease pan
  • Vanilla flavoring

Instructions:

  1. Wash berries well and strain. Then mash in a large-holed strainer to get out most of the seeds
  2. Make a custard with the yolks of three eggs, sugar, cornstarch, and vanilla flavoring
    1. Mix cornstarch and sugar
    2. Place mixture over boiling water (in double boiler), stirring constantly until it begins to thicken
    3. Cover and then let cook for 10 minutes and beat egg yolks
    4. Stir 1/2 cup of mixture into the beaten eggs, and mix in gradually
    5. Repeat step 2(d) 3 times or more until complete
    6. Stir well and cook entire mix for 2 more minutes
    7. Remove from heat and once cooled, add vanilla extract
  3. Mix custard and berries and pour in a deep baking dish which as been greased
  4. Make a meringue from 3 egg whites stiffly beaten using 3 egg whites amd 3 tbsp sugar
    1. Beat egg whites until they are broken up and frothy
    2. Beat to soft peaks, then gradually add the sugar until it has been fully incorporated
    3. Beat until glossy, stiff peaks form
  5. Brown in moderate oven

Here is a recipe from the kitchen of Mrs. R. A. Holloman, Jr. It was used for many years by the family of her mother, Mrs. H. U. Griffith. This was a favorite dessert with the family with the exception of one uncle who was always heard to say whenever the dish was served, "This dish certainly has the right name because it is trifling." My mother would always reply with, "Well, the proof of the pudding is in the taste thereof."
