Hummingbird Cake - Desserts

Ingredients:

  • Cake Mix:
    • 2 cups chopped pecans (roasted)
    • 3 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 tsp baking soda
    • 1-1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
    • 1/2 tsp allspice
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 2 cups mashed bananas (4 ripe bananas, preferably slightly brown)
    • one 8 oz can crushed pineapple, with juice
    • 3 large eggs at room temperature
    • 2/3 cup vegetable/canola oil (or coconut oil)
    • 1 cup packed light (or dark) brown sugar
    • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Frosting:
    • two 8-oz blocks of cream cheese, softened to room temperature
    • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
    • 5 cups of confectionary sugar
    • 1 tbsp milk
    • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
    • 1/8 tsp salt

Instructions:

  1. Prep
    1. Preheat oven to 300.
    2. Spread pecans onto a lined baking pan and toast for 8 minutes
    3. Turn oven up to 350 and grease and lightly flour three 9-inch cake pans
  2. Cake mix
    1. Whisk together the flour, baking soda, cinnamon, allspice, and salt together in a large bowl
    2. Whisk the bananas, crushed pineapple, eggs, vegetable oil, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract in a medium mixing bowl until completely combined
    3. Combine the two mixes into the larger of the bowls, mix well
    4. Fold in 1-1/2 cups of the pecans (save the other half for garnish)
    5. Spread the batter between the 3 cake pans and bake for 26-29 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean
    6. Rotate the pans halfway through baking
    7. Remove the pans from the oven and allow the cakes to cool completely on a wire rack. Once cooled, remove the cakes and level off the tops.
  3. Frosting
    1. Beat the cream cheese and butter at a high speed until smooth and creamy
    2. Add the confectionary sugar, vanilla, milk, and salt. Beat on a low speed for 30 seconds and then a high speed for 2 minutes.
  4. Assembly
    1. Place one layer of the cake on your serving plate and evenly cover the top with frosting. Repeat for the remaining 2 layers.
    2. Spread the remaining frosting over the top and down the sides.
    3. Garnish with remaining 1-1/2 cup of toasted pecans
    4. Refrigerate for 30 minutes before slicing

Source: Southern Living - Hummingbird Cake - Mrs. L.H. Wiggins of Greensboro (1978)
