Alumni Field - Lexington, VA

The land of Alumni Field (sometimes Alumni Memorial Field) has been used by the Virginia Military Institute since its completion in 1921. It replaced the usage of their Parade Grounds as a playing field. It has also been called Foster Stadium at Alumni Field since 2006.

Overall, NC State had a record of 1-0 at this field.

Alumni Field opened in 1921 with 4,000 seats; following the 1961 season, the field underwent a serious renovation, expanding the seating to 10,000 seats. Some people refer to this improved stadium as Alumni Field II to differentiate it from the former, smaller, field. In 2006, the stadium was improved again, receiving new entranceways, new concourses and restrooms, upgraded seating and a new scoreboard, and new locker rooms. The improvements were made thanks to the contributions of P. Wesley Foster, an alumni and former football player from VMI's class of 1956; afterwards, the field was renamed Alumni Memorial Field at Foster Stadium, in honor of the stadium's primary benefactor [1] [2].
