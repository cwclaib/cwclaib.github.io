Baujan Stadium - Dayton, OH

Baujan Stadium was used by Dayton from 1925-1973; it replaced Founders Field and was replaced by Welcome Field, a shared venue off of Dayton's campus that opened in 1949.

Overall, NC State has a record of 1-0 at this field, winning their sole game.

Baujan Field opened in 1925 as Dayton Field; in 1961, the venue was renamed in honor of Harry Baujan, a former coach and athletic director at Dayton. In 1974, after football moved to Welcome Field, the venue's original concrete bleachers, which sat 12,000, were torn down and the venue was converted to a soccer field [1] [2].
