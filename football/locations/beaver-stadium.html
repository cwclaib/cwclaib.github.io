Beaver Stadium - State College, PA

Beaver Stadium has been used by Penn State since 1960; it replaced New Beaver Field, which served as Penn State's home field from 1909-1959.

Overall, NC State had a record of 1-8 at this field, losing the one game they were ranked beforehand and posting a 1-6 record when Penn State was ranked.

While Beaver Stadium was built as a replacement for New Beaver Field, it wasn't just a replacement; it was also a relocation -- the entire 30,000-seat bleachers were moved from one end of campus to the other. In 1969, the bleachers were expanded to 46,284, and just three years later, the seating was increased by almost ten thousand. The seating has been increased another seven times since 1972, reaching a maximum of 107,282 from 2001-2011; it now seats slightly fewer, at 106,572.

Perhaps the most major renovation to the stadium was the removal of the track in 1978. As a part of the replacement, which increased the venue's seating by 16,000, and the entire bowl of the stadium was lifted on hydraulic jacks to make the addition. An upper deck was added prior to the 1991 football season [1].
