Bryant-Denny Stadium - Tuscaloosa, AL

Bryant-Denny Stadium has been used by Alabama since 1929; it replaced Denny Field, which was used from 1915 to 1928.

Overall, NC State had a record of 0-4 at this field, losing all their games as non-conference games.

Denny Field opened in September 1929 with a capacity of 12,000. Prior to the 1937 season, Denny Field's capacity was doubled by the construction of permanent seating on both sides of the field. In 1946, bleachers to hold 4,000 were added to the end zones, while improvements to the west grandstand (in 1961) and the completion of the end zone bleachers and improvements to the east grandstand (in 1966) brought the stadium to 43,000 seats and 60,210 seats respectively.

Because of the stadium's relatively small size during this time, many of Alabama's most important home games were actually played in Birmingham's Legion Field, which had capacity for several thousand more fans in any given year. This started to change in 1988, when the west side bleachers received an upper deck, raising the seating capacity to 70,123. In 1998 and 1999, the east side stands received an upper deck, as well as a series luxury boxes, providing room for 83,818 as well as an agreement with the local cities to cease holding games in Birmingham. The north end zone received an expansion from 2004-2006, and the south end zone in 2010, pumping the capacity from 92,138 to 101,821 [1] [2].
