[1]
 NC State did not field her first team until the Spring of 1892, and did not play intercollegiate games until 1893. NC State also may not have fielded a team in 1896.
[2]
 Both UNC and Duke claim wins by forfeit in this game. Though the game was meant to take place after the Thanksgiving Day Duke-Wake Forest game, Duke reportedly wrote to UNC saying "We will play you if our men are not too badly broken up by the Wake Forest game." The game was never played (Barrier, p. 22). The NC Inter-Collegiate Foot-Ball Association officially ruled the season a split decision (Wilmington Messenger, February 16th, 1890, p. 1).
[3]
 As Duke was the only team in the state to play in 1890, they are the technical champion. The same is the case for UNC's 1896 team.
[4]
 UNC's faculty banned intercollegiate football in 1890; North Carolina also did not field a team in 1917-18, though some summaries include North Carolina's Student Army Training Corps (SATC) record from 1918.
[5]
 Wake Forest did not field a team in 1890, apparently due to lack of interest (Wake Forest Student, Vol. X No. 1 (October 1890), p. 30). Wake Forest also failed to field a team in 1894 and 1896-1907 after faculy bans on the sport.
[6]
 Both UNC and Wake Forest claim wins by forfeit for this game. On November 11th, 1891, UNC and Wake Forest were playing in Raleigh; the game was described by Perrin Busbee as, at the time, "probably the best and most scientific game of foot ball... that has ever been played in Raleigh." Wake Forest had the ball and was down 6-4 when UNC was called for "throttling." UNC's team disagreed so strongly with the call that they left the field in protest, claiming that the umpire, Prince, "did not know foot ball rules" (News and Observer, November 12th, 1891, p. 2).
[7]
 NC State did play a "picked" (mostly scrub) team from UNC twice, losing the first game 0-22 but winning the second 8-6.
[8]
 Duke faculty banned intercollegiate football from 1894-1919.
[9]
 Arguably, this title could go to North Carolina by comparative losses; UNC tied Virginia Tech, the two teams' only shared opponent, 0-0, while NC State lost 2-18.
